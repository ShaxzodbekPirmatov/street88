A hamburger is a type of sandwich consisting of a fried chopped patty served inside a sliced round bun. It is usually complemented with a variety of seasonings, for example, lettuce, ketchup and mayonnaise, pickled cucumber, tomato or zucchini slices, raw or fried onions.

As the most common fast food in the US, the hamburger is far from American in origin. Scientists attribute its authorship to the nomadic Scythians, who put fried meat between two slices of bread.
